How it builds

Left alone, the system chokes.

Minerals crystallize into scale. Steel oxidizes into rust. Grease films harden. Biofilm takes hold. Layer by layer the walls close in, and every layer costs energy, capacity, and safety.

The conventional answer

The fix is chemistry, and chemistry gets scored.

Crews fight buildup with acids, caustics, and biocides. Industry rates the danger of each one with HMIS, the Hazardous Materials Identification System: three numbers, each from 0 to 4.

Health Can it hurt you? 0 means no risk. 4 means life-threatening on brief exposure.
Flammability Will it ignite? 0 means it will not burn. 4 means it ignites at room temperature.
Reactivity Is it stable? 0 means stable. 4 means it can detonate under the wrong conditions.
Every chemical gets all three scores on this scale0 = none  →  4 = severe
