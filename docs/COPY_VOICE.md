# MASEST / VertKleen — Copy Voice Guide

The contract for all site prose. Approved by owner 2026-06-25. Style pass only — **never change a fact** (HMIS 0-0-0, CAGE 0B2Q3, named numbers, claims, product names).

## Who reads it
Plant maintenance leads, facility managers, hospital/school/brewery/marine operations, procurement officers, distributors. Practical, skeptical, time-poor, accountable for both safety and budget. They think in equipment, drums, dilution, downtime, OSHA, dollars, and what actually works.

## The voice
**Plain industrial English, confident because the product earns it.**

VertKleen cleans as hard as the acids and caustics it replaces, at an HMIS 0-0-0 safety rating. The proof is real (20-yr rust in 30 min, beat CLR, brewery CIP, ~$10k/yr saved at a Walmart DC). So the copy can carry a little swagger — but the swagger comes from the number, never from a punchy fragment.

## Answer the buyer's six questions
What is it · what does it replace · is it safe · does it work · what's it cost · how do I buy. If a sentence answers none of these, cut it.

## Do
- Concrete nouns: drums, coils, towers, rust, scale, grease, downtime, dollars, eyewash station.
- Lead with the benefit, back it with the receipt: "Cleared 20-year rust in 30 minutes where CLR ran 36 hours and quit."
- Vary sentence length. A real sentence, then a short one. Fragments allowed but rationed.
- Keep it pasteable into a procurement justification — professional, specific, no hype words.

## Don't
- **Process-noun jargon** — the #1 problem. Ban as filler: *route / routed / routing, scope (as noun), profile, file, map (as verb), burden, drag, friction, overhead, roster, canonical.* Say the real thing: "ships without hazmat freight," not "lower-friction shipping profile."
- **"Not X but Y" fragment headlines.** One earned contrast per page, max — and only when someone genuinely holds X. Kill: "The dirt is obvious. The hazard hides in the drum." / "Read the invoice, not the label." / "No showroom demos. Working equipment."
- **Personified-fragment headers:** "Buildup does not ask permission." / "Your current chemical has an exit plan."
- **Rule-of-three chants** and hype words (revolutionary, seamless, game-changing, lower-burden).

## Worked example — homepage hero
Before: "What maintenance is up against / The dirt is obvious. The hazard hides in the drum. / VertKleen keeps the industrial job while replacing harsh acids, caustics, solvents, and biocides with lower-burden chemistry."

After: **Industrial cleaning power. None of the hazard.** / VertKleen strips scale, rust, grease, and biofilm as hard as the acids and caustics on your shelf today — at an HMIS 0-0-0 safety rating. Same clean. No burns, no fumes, no hazmat freight.

## CTA verbs
"Find your replacement," "Request a trial," "Get a quote," "Become a distributor." Plain imperatives. Not "Scope a Trial."
